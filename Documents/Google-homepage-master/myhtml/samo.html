Chinua Achebe

Africa's greatest storyteller

Chinua Achebe speaking at Asbury Hall, Buffalo, as part of the "Babel: Season 2" series by Just Buffalo Literary Center, Hallwalls, & the International Institute.

The life of Chinua Achebe:

  • 1930 - Born Albert Chinụalụmọgụ Achebe in the Igbo village of Ogidi on the 16th of November.
  • 1936 - Entered St Philips' Central School.
  • 1944 - Sat for entrance examinations and was accepted.
  • 1948 - Obtained such high marks in the entrance examination of the University College (now the University of Ibadan) that he was admitted as a Major Scholar in the university's first intake and given a bursary to study medicine but He later abandoned the study of medicine and changed to English, history, and theology.
  • 1950 - Wrote a piece for the University Herald entitled "Polar Undergraduate", his debut as an author.
  • 1953 - Was awarded a second-class degree and later took up a teaching job at Merchants of Light school at Oba.
  • 1954 - Started work for the Nigerian Broadcasting Service(NBS) in Lagos.
  • 1958 - Heinemann published 2,000 hardcover copies of Achebe's book "Things Fall Apart" on the 17th of June and he was later promoted at the NBS and put in charge of the network's eastern region coverage.
  • 1960 - Wrote a novel titled "No Longer at Ease".
  • 1961 - Got married to Christiana Chinwe (Christie) Okoli on the 10th of September
  • 1966 - Published his first children's book, Chike and the River.
  • 1971 - Wrote "Dirge for Okigbo", originally in the Igbo language but later translated to English.
  • 1972 = Released his book "Girls at War" a collection of short stories ranging in time from his undergraduate days to the recent bloodshed. It was the 100th book in Heinemann's African Writers Series, The University of Massachusetts Amherst offered Achebe a professorship later that year.
  • 1975 - Presented a Chancellor's Lecture at Amherst.
  • 1976 - Returned to the University of Nigeria to accomplish three goals: finish the novel he had been writing, renew the native publication of Okike, and further his study of Igbo culture.
  • 1979 - Was awarded the first-ever Nigerian National Merit Award
  • 1982 - Retired from the University of Nigeria.
  • 1983 - Became the party's deputy national vice-president. He published a book called The Trouble with Nigeria to coincide with the upcoming elections.
  • 1986 - Was elected president-general of the Ogidi Town Union; he reluctantly accepted and began a three-year term. In the same year, he stepped down as editor of Okike.
  • 1990 - Was riding in a car to Lagos when an axle collapsed and the car flipped; the weight of the vehicle fell on Achebe and his spine was severely damaged which confined him to a wheelchair for the rest of his life.
  • 2007 - Was awarded the Man Booker International Prize.
  • 2012 - Achebe's publishers, Penguin Books, released There Was a Country: A Personal History of Biafra. Publication immediately caused a stir and re-opened the discussion about the Nigerian Civil War. It would prove to be the last publication during his lifetime.
  • 2013 - Died at the age of 83 after a short illness on 21st of March in Boston, United States.
